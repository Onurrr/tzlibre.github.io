FAQ

Splitting

How can I split?

Using contribution data and DLS tools you will need to build your private key. We strongly suggest to do so on an airgapped machine, or at a least on a machine that is temporarily disconnected. You will then need to sign a message to prove ownership of your address and explicit support of the TzLibre fork. Finally, you'll need to broadcast the signed message. Before splitting, we suggest you to do your own research on how coin splits and cryptographic signatures work. Also, we suggest you to bookmark our official web and Telegram addresses. We're currently upgrading the splitting process to make it fully trustless and pseudonymous and will announce it on our public Telegram channel.

What happens after the split?

After split you will receive the TZL pre-launch token. The pre-launch token will be converted into the TzLibre mainnet token at time of launch.

Where can I find more details about the split?

We're currently upgrading the splitting process to make it fully trustless and pseudonymous. Updates will be announced on our public Telegram channel.

Will I lose XTZ if I split?

No. Splitting allows a Tezos contributor to claim TZL at a 1:1 ratio with XTZ.

Will every Tezos contributor get TZL?

No. Only contributors who split will get TZL coins. We want to safeguard the financial interest of TzLibre users.

Code

How can you fork DLS-Tezos if it's not open-source?

The DLS-Tezos codebase is public on Gitlab, and we already forked it. Arthur Breitman promised the DLS-Tezos codebase shall be released under the MIT license immediately after launch. Should we decide to launch earlier than them, or should DLS-Tezos not be release their code under the MIT license, we'll then choose one of the many options to release TzLibre. We might release a patch to their public codebase: a TzLibre user would therefore download the public DLS-Tezos codebase from Gitlab, and then locally apply the TzLibre patch. And let us be frank: we find the idea of a closed-source blockchain utterly ludicrous.

What did you fork?

We forked the public DLS-Tezos repo that is available on Gitlab.

Where is your code?

We recently made a PR to fix the broken DLS-Tezos fundraiser tools. It can be found on our Github page. Please follow us there to get updates as we gradually release more code to the public.

Are you going to use DLS-Tezos genesis?

No. The TZL pre-launch token, as in the EOS example, is a placeholder for the TzLibre genesis.

Why won't you use DLS-Tezos genesis?

We need to be totally independent from DLS-Tezos genesis mainly for two reasons: unknown launch date, and blinded genesis. Since Tezos contribution data is available on the public BTC and ETH blockchains we don't really need to depend upon their genesis block.

Does TzLibre have the same tx and address format as DLS-Tezos?

No. TzLibre has a different address format, and a different tx format.

Funding

How are you currently funded?

We're currently self-funded.

How will you fund further development?

We will fund ongoing development of TzLibre with donations. We plan most donations to come from Tezos Stiftung.

Why should Tezos Stiftung fund the development of TzLibre?

As our legal advisors have confirmed, Tezos Stiftung must follow Swiss law and abide to its immutable charter by supporting development of the Tezos idea. We understand that while Tezos is an idea, DLS-Tezos and TzLibre are two different implementations of it. Should Tezos Stiftung choose not to abide to its immutable charter, any Swiss contributor might then bring the case toward a Swiss judge. To increase legal leverage for our case, coin splitting will involve cryptographically signing a pseudonymous, authentic, non-repudiable request to Tezos Stiftung to fund TzLibre.

Growth

How will TzLibre grow after launch?

After launch, growth will be mainly driven by bakers, developers and investors moving from DLS-Tezos to TzLibre.

Why should bakers leave DLS-Tezos to join TzLibre?

At least 20% of TZL coins will be allotted to reward bakers. If less than 100% of Tezos ICO contributors redeem their TZL, we plan to increase bakers rewards with unredeemed coins. We predict that, in the first 3-5 years, baking on TzLibre will be 2-5x more profitable than baking on DLS-Tezos (~5%/y ROI).

Why should developers deploy smart contracts on TzLibre rather than DLS-Tezos?

TzLibre was announced in February 2018, while DLS-Tezos was announced in August 2014. TzLibre is a much younger project and we therefore expect, ceteris paribus, the price of TZL coins to be cheaper than XTZ coins for the first 2-3 years. The underlying technology of the two blockchains is the same (both blockchains can execute Michelson smart contracts), while TzLibre exhibits stronger security features. We therefore expect developers to deploy contracts on TzLibre in order to build on a safer platform, at a fraction of the cost.

Why should investors sell XTZ to buy TZL?

Both TzLibre and DLS-Tezos share the same underslying technology and its technological risk. While the downside is comparable, the upside of the two investments is widely different. We expect TZL coins to price lower than XTZ during the first months of trading. Last but not least, TZL is a better store of value than XTZ thanks to its hardcoded, non-negotiable hard cap.

Team

Why are you pseudonymous?

We are pseudonymous to protect the independence of Tezos Libre. No one shall ever coerce Tezos Libre developers. We believe state sanctioned identities belong to the fiat world, not to the blockchain world. Like Satoshi Nakamoto, we believe in permissionless, open, pseudonymous innovation.

How can I trust you?

You should not. We believe in the original blockchain ethos: trust no one, think by yourself, verify everything. We are aware that TzLibre is not for everyone. If you need a trusted third party to use a blockchain, then TzLibre is not for you.

What is your development team?

We have two small development teams with different expertise: a team of blockchain veteran developers, and an academic team of functional programming experts.

Why don't you propose a protocol upgrade to DLS-Tezos instead of forking it?

We believe DLS-Tezos is starting from wrong assumptions. It'd be extremely hard for a trusted chain to "become trustless". We can't see how that might happen, and we're fed up with DLS delays. We believe it's smarter, faster and more efficient to fork and start from scratch. Nevertheless, we'd love to see a Libre-like proposal to DLS-Tezos: veto power should be removed, premined coins to DLS and TF should be destroyed, money supply should be capped. We'd love to see that happen, but we find it very unlikely.

Are you against DLS-Tezos?

No, quite the opposite. We are Tezos contributors, and we are set to explore a different path toward success, putting ICO contributors interest first. This is a way, for us, to hedge our bets and not be totally dependent upon DLS. As with ETH and ETC, we see DLS-Tezos and TzLibre as complementary. Last but not least, we are putting healthy pressure on DLS to improve, making sure they don't sit on their monopoly. We're giving them a run for their money.

Why are you doing it?

We launched TzLibre for idealistic as well as financial reasons. As anarcho-capitalists we find the two deeply connected. Tezos is an idea and it should be implemented by multiple, competing teams. Many contributors are extremely dissatisfied with DLS execution. We believe DLS is not only executing poorly, but also totally violating the original, libertarian blockchain ethos. We are Tezos contributors ourselves. We're also aiming to double our stake and hedge our bets between XTZ and TZL. Last but not least, we are putting healthy pressure on DLS to improve, making sure they don't sit on their monopoly. We're giving them a run for their money.

Will the fork benefit Tezos contributors?

Yes. Contributors directly benefit from the fork, as they can double their stake and hedge their bets. Both DLS-Tezos and TzLibre work toward the same goal: implementing a decentralized, self-governing, digital commonwealth.
